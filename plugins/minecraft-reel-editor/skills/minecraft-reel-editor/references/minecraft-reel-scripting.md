# Minecraft reel scripting guide

## Goal

Build one exact-word reel under 30 seconds from material already spoken in the recording. The reel should feel intentional even when the source is a five-minute pile of mistakes and retakes.

## Story shape

Choose one idea and arrange it as:

1. **Hook:** Begin with the most specific surprise, risk, result, contradiction, or challenge. Skip greetings and throat-clearing.
2. **Setup:** Keep only the context required to understand what is happening in Minecraft.
3. **Escalation:** Retain the shortest clean explanation or action that creates anticipation.
4. **Payoff:** End on the result, reveal, failure, joke, or lesson. Do not continue explaining after the payoff.

Favor a strong later sentence as the opening when it works without invented context.

## Selecting takes

- Treat phrases such as “wait,” “no,” “let me restart,” and a sentence repeated from the beginning as retake signals unless they are clearly part of the story.
- Prefer a later take when it cleanly corrects an earlier one, but judge clarity and energy rather than automatically choosing the last take.
- Keep complete natural phrases. Avoid cuts inside words, contractions, or tightly connected clauses.
- Remove apologies, production talk, dead explanations, repeated facts, and abandoned sentences.
- Keep a stumble only when it is genuinely funny and strengthens the reel.
- Never combine isolated words from unrelated takes to create wording the speaker did not naturally deliver.

## Minecraft-specific judgment

- Keep the crosshair and central action as the default visual anchor.
- Use `full_frame` when meaning depends on inventory slots, chat, coordinates, side HUD, a sign, scoreboard, or an off-center build.
- Preserve a pause when the viewer needs to watch a block placement, explosion, mob reaction, reveal, death, or redstone result.
- Do not cut away before the visual payoff completes.
- Avoid packing multiple unrelated builds, tips, or stories into one reel.

## Pacing

- Aim for 18–28 seconds and fail the proposal above 30 seconds.
- Use `compress` for accidental gaps longer than roughly half a second.
- Use `preserve` for visual or comedic timing.
- Retain short breaths and emphasis. The target is fast but human, not a wall of syllables.

## Captions

- Caption exact spoken words.
- Use short phrase cards of two to five words.
- Highlight the currently spoken word in Minecraft yellow.
- Keep captions away from the bottom interaction controls and away from critical HUD elements where possible.

## Proposal quality bar

Before asking for approval, verify:

- every proposed word exists in a selected contiguous transcript range;
- the first sentence works as a hook;
- the viewer can understand the setup without removed context;
- repeated or failed takes are absent;
- the ending lands on the payoff;
- crop modes protect important gameplay information;
- the estimated rendered duration is below 30 seconds.
